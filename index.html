3D Visualization API for Self-Driving Cars (2017)

Abstract: In the current, self-driving cars are a huge topic especially because quality and safety are important factors for humanity. The mortality caused by traffic accidents due to human errors is relatively high. Research topics revolving around autonomous driving and the technology involved with it have made their appearance, as it has gained in importance but also in complexity.

The 3D visualization of research progress is useful so that the software, which controls the autonomous car, can be simulated and tested in graphical form before using it in a real life scenario. This bachelor thesis deals with the 3D visualization of self-driving cars in web browsers. The graphical representation of the car behavior helps to better understand the mechanism of the controller and offers a visual overview about it. Besides the visualization of the car, it also tackles the question on how to remotely control a car rendered in a browser.

An ethics guideline for data scientists (2020)

Abstract: In recent years, data science has become an indispensable part of our society. Over time, we have become reliant on this technology because of its opportunity to gain value and new insights from data in any field - business, socializing, research and society. The first phase of data science focused on the accuracy and efficiency of data and its algorithms. The second phase is now based on the growing dependency - due to convergence of big data, cloud computing, the use of new technologies, such as artificial intelligence (AI), machine learning algorithms, which allows automation for some tasks with better performance than humans. At the same time, it raises questions about how justified we are in placing our trust in these technologies. There is a risk that such powers may lead to biased, inappropriate or unintended actions. Automated decisions by AI may not be transparent and unfair towards certain groups. Each step in the data science process (from raw data to insight and knowledge retrieval) may create bias. Therefore, ethical considerations which might occur as the result of data science practices should be carefully considered and these potential problems should be identified during the data science lifecycle and mitigated if possible. This leads to the area of responsible data science (RDS). Aspects of RDS centers around data ethics, e.g. achieving fair, consensual, transparent data which shall provide clarity to data science algorithms. There are some tools towards data ethics, e.g. ethical codes by the ACM, the IEEE and the ASA that address data. But these theories have to be embedded in practical approaches. Although various researchers have raised ethical concerns in AI, there have been very few studies on how data science practice can address these concerns. A typical data scientist has not enough knowledge for identifying these challenges and it is not always possible to include an ethics expert during data science production. So, in this thesis, we provide a practical guideline to data scientists and increase their awareness. We reviewed different sources of biases and mapped them under different stages of the data science lifecycle. More concrete, we have analyzed the data science pipeline and its stages. There are various different models, but we described each phase of a specific model using user stories - from the data ingestion phase to the data analysis phase. For each phase, we analyzed emerging biases and described them with definitions, examples and best practice. With this knowledge we then identified a vocabulary to define concepts. After that, we created a supporting ontology. Therefore, we analyzed existing ontologies and selected specific classes to create our own ontology on data science and ethics. With our ontology it is possible to extract knowledge from a specific domain. It is possible to write queries, which for instance extract particular methods for a certain bias. Furthermore it is possible to analyze and extend different types of biases. As an extension to this, there will be the possibility to generate a knowledge graph from certain keywords, which for example indicates for a bias in which phase it can be retrieved, which mitigation method can be applied, how it relates to other biases and similar. Finally, we have implemented and analyzed a development tool to support data scientists be aware of existing biases in the data science lifecycle. With the help of the guiding tool it is possible to explore all phases of data science and all emerging biases. Furthermore, data scientists can tag their code with a vocabulary. Finally, the developed tool is evaluated by computer scientist for its usability and user experience.
